Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us recognize you, remember your preferences, and understand how you use our website. We use both session-based and persistent cookies to enhance your experience and gather information about how our website is used.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for website functionality, security, and basic operations
  • Performance Cookies: Help us understand website usage and improve performance
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and personalize your experience
  • Marketing Cookies: Track your interactions for advertising and marketing purposes

Third-Party Tracking

We may use third-party analytics and marketing tools such as Google Analytics, Google Ads, Facebook Pixel, and similar services to track user behavior, measure campaign performance, and deliver targeted advertisements. These third parties may also place cookies on your device. You can opt out of many third-party tracking services through your browser settings or by visiting opt-out pages provided by these services.

Managing Cookies

Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. You can also typically delete cookies from your device. However, blocking or deleting cookies may affect your ability to use certain features of our website. For more information about managing cookies, visit your browser's help section or www.aboutcookies.org.

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. However, we may share your information in the following circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We share information with vendors and contractors who assist us in providing services, subject to confidentiality agreements

  • Aggregated Data: We may share anonymized, aggregated data that cannot identify you personally

While we strive to protect your information, no security system is impenetrable. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your data. You acknowledge that you provide information at your own risk.
